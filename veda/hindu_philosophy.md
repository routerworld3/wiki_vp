
## Big Picture

Hindu philosophy has many schools, but the most discussed are **Vedānta schools**, because they interpret the **Upaniṣads, Bhagavad Gītā, and Brahma Sūtra**.

| Philosophy                    |            Main Teacher |                                     Time | Main Idea                                                                            |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------: | ---------------------------------------: | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Advaita Vedānta**           |        Ādi Śaṅkarācārya |                           8th century CE | Brahman alone is ultimately real; soul and Brahman are ultimately one                |
| **Viśiṣṭādvaita**             |           Rāmānujācārya |                     11th–12th century CE | Brahman is one, but with real qualities; souls and world are real parts/modes of God |
| **Dvaita Vedānta**            |             Madhvācārya |                          13th century CE | God, soul, and world are eternally distinct                                          |
| **Dvaitādvaita / Bhedābheda** |           Nimbārkācārya | often placed around 12th–13th century CE | Soul and world are both different and non-different from God                         |
| **Śuddhādvaita**              |           Vallabhācārya |                     15th–16th century CE | Brahman/Kṛṣṇa alone is real; world is real manifestation of God, not illusion        |
| **Achintya Bhedābheda**       | Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu |                     15th–16th century CE | Soul and God are simultaneously one and different in an inconceivable way            |

## 1) Advaita Vedānta — Non-dualism

**Propagated by:** Ādi Śaṅkarācārya
**Time:** 8th century CE
**Main deity focus:** Nirguṇa Brahman, though Śaṅkara also composed devotional hymns.

**Key points**

* Ultimate reality is **Brahman**: formless, attributeless, non-dual.
* Individual soul **Ātman = Brahman** at the highest level.
* The world is not absolutely unreal, but **dependent/temporary**, often explained through **māyā**.
* Liberation means realizing: **“I am not body/mind; I am Brahman.”**

Simple formula:
**God and soul are ultimately one.**

---

## 2) Viśiṣṭādvaita — Qualified Non-dualism

**Propagated by:** Śrī Rāmānujācārya
**Time:** 1017–1137 CE
**Main deity focus:** Nārāyaṇa / Viṣṇu

**Key points**

* Brahman is personal: **Nārāyaṇa**.
* Soul and world are real, not illusion.
* Soul and world are part of God, like body is related to soul.
* Liberation comes through **bhakti and surrender**.

Simple formula:
**God is one, but souls and world are real parts of Him.**

---

## 3) Dvaita Vedānta — Dualism

**Propagated by:** Śrī Madhvācārya
**Time:** 1238–1317 CE
**Main deity focus:** Viṣṇu / Nārāyaṇa

**Key points**

* God, soul, and world are eternally different.
* Viṣṇu is supreme and independent.
* Souls are dependent on God.
* Liberation means eternal service to God, not merging with Him.

Simple formula:
**God and soul are always different.**

---

## 4) Bhedābheda / Dvaitādvaita — Difference and Non-difference

**Propagated by:** Śrī Nimbārkācārya
**Time:** often placed around 12th–13th century CE
**Main deity focus:** Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa

**Key points**

* Soul and world are both different from and non-different from God.
* Example: sun and sunlight.

  * Sunlight is not separate from the sun.
  * But sunlight is not the full sun either.
* Devotion to Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa is central.

Simple formula:
**Soul is part of God, but not equal to God.**

---

## 5) Śuddhādvaita — Pure Non-dualism

**Propagated by:** Śrī Vallabhācārya
**Time:** 1479–1531 CE
**Main deity focus:** Śrī Kṛṣṇa

**Key points**

* Brahman is Kṛṣṇa.
* The world is real, not illusion.
* The world is a real manifestation of God.
* Emphasis on loving service, grace, and devotion.

Simple formula:
**Everything is real because everything is God’s manifestation.**

---

## 6) Achintya Bhedābheda — Inconceivable Oneness and Difference

**Propagated by:** Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu
**Time:** 1486–1534 CE
**Main deity focus:** Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa

**Key points**

* Soul and God are simultaneously one and different.
* This relationship is **achintya**: beyond ordinary logic.
* God is the complete whole; soul is His eternal part.
* Highest path is **bhakti**, especially chanting God’s names.

Simple formula:
**Soul is one with God as energy, but different as individual person.**

## Quick Comparison

| Question         | Advaita                       | Viśiṣṭādvaita      | Dvaita      | Bhedābheda / Gauḍiya     |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------ | ----------- | ------------------------ |
| Is world real?   | Relatively real, not ultimate | Real               | Real        | Real                     |
| Is soul God?     | Ultimately yes                | Part/mode of God   | No          | One and different        |
| Is God personal? | Ultimately beyond form        | Yes                | Yes         | Yes                      |
| Liberation means | Realizing Brahman             | Serving God        | Serving God | Loving devotion to Kṛṣṇa |
| Main path        | Knowledge                     | Bhakti + surrender | Bhakti      | Bhakti                   |

---

## One-line summary

* **Advaita:** only Brahman is ultimate.
* **Viśiṣṭādvaita:** God is one whole; souls/world are real parts.
* **Dvaita:** God and soul are eternally different.
* **Bhedābheda:** soul is both different and non-different from God.
* **Achintya Bhedābheda:** this oneness/difference is real but beyond full human logic.
